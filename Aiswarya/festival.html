Festivals In India

Onam, Holi, and Diwali are all celebrated Indian festivals, but they differ significantly: Onam is Kerala's harvest festival with flower carpets and boat races, Holi is a spring festival of colors, and Diwali is the "Festival of Lights" marked by lamp-lighting, fireworks, and gift-giving, observed by multiple religious communities

DIWALI:THE FESTIVAL OF LIGHTS

Diwali, or Deepavali, is a vibrant five-day "Festival of Lights" celebrated by Hindus, Jains, Sikhs, and some Buddhists, symbolizing the victory of good over evil and light over darkness. Originating from ancient India, the festival involves lighting oil lamps, decorating homes with colourful patterns called rangoli, exchanging gifts and sweets, and feasting. The specific traditions and stories vary by faith and region, but a common thread is the emphasis on family, community, prosperity, and the celebration of new beginnings

HOLI:A JOYFUL AND COLORFUL FESTIVAL

Holi is the Hindu "Festival of Colours," a vibrant, joyful celebration marking the arrival of spring and signifying the victory of good over eviljoyful. It involves a night of Holika Dahan (bonfires) and a day of playing with coloured powders and water. Rooted in mythology, particularly the story of Prahlad and Holika, and the legend of Krishna and Radha, Holi also represents love, unity, and new beginnings

ONAM:THE HARVEST FESTIVAL OF KERALA

Onam is an annual Hindu harvest festival in the Indian state of Kerala, celebrated during the Malayalam month of Chingam (August-Septemberto commemorate the homecoming of the mythical King Mahabali. The ten-day festival features floral arrangements called Pookalam, a grand feast known as Onasadya, traditional boat races called Vallam Kali, mask dances known as Kummattikali, and other cultural events, symbolizing prosperity, unity, and Kerala's rich cultural heritage>
